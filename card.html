White Tiger

White tigers are not a separate species but a rare color variant of Bengal tigers:

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris tigris
  • Average Length: 8 to 10 feet (2.4 to 3 meters)
  • Average Lifespan: 10 to 15 years
  • Habitat: Dense forests, mangroves, and grasslands of India.

White tigers are a rare color variation of Bengal tigers, distinguished by their striking white fur with black or dark brown stripes and icy blue eyes. This unique appearance is the result of a recessive gene mutation, and most white tigers found today are bred in captivity.
